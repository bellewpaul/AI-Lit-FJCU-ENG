Course Description

In this class, we examine the literature of Artificial Intelligence. We analyze fiction, poetry, and film written by, in collaboration with, or about AI.

In addition to literary texts, we explore non-fiction sources — news articles, academic papers — tracing what "AI" means and how it works: from its foundations in simple language games through its development into complex chatbots today.

This class involves creative writing assignments using AI systems, studying how AI generates text, and building our own text generators. It is interdisciplinary: you will read, analyze, interpret, write, and work with code.

You must use an AI chatbot this semester. Choose one, stick with it. Prof. Paul recommends Claude.

Major Assignments

Literary Interpretation Paper
Thesis-Based Analytical Essay
Analyze a literary text generated by or created in collaboration with AI. You may use primary source readings from the semester or find your own (check with Prof. Paul). The essay must interpret the text and its publication medium.

BA: ≥1,000 words · 1 peer-reviewed source
MA: ≥1,500 words · 2 peer-reviewed sources

Note: AI may only be used to revise sentences. Do not use AI to generate new writing or offload deep thinking.
AI-Collaborative Creative Writing
Design a Creative Writing Process
Collaborate with AI to produce a creative text in any form — poetry, short story, novel, play, screenplay, etc. Part of the text must be algorithmically generated. You may edit freely.

Part 1: Finished text (5,000–100,000 words) at professional quality
Part 2: Process description ≥1,000 words

Simply asking a chatbot to write for you will not pass. Your process must be as involved as the GPT-2 fine-tuning exercise.
AI Chat Logs
Weekly Logs + Final Reflection
Submit all course-related chat logs weekly (copied text + one screenshot). Personal, unrelated chats do not need to be submitted.

At semester end, write a ≥300-word reflection on how you used AI: what worked, what didn't, whether you overused it, and how it affected your learning.
Creative Project Presentation
Present Your Collaborative Work
Present your AI-Collaborative Creative Writing Project to the class during the final weeks. Presentations take place May 22 through June 12. Sign up for your slot.
